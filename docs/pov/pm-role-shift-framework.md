# The PM Role-Shift Framework

> Valtech's opinionated point of view on how the Product Manager role is changing —
> and how to tell genuine change from hype.
>
> This is the **authored asset** at the heart of breezemerchant. The LLM does not
> invent this model; it maps live client context onto it. Edit this file to sharpen
> the POV — the product gets smarter as this gets sharper.

---

## The two halves of the model

breezemerchant works by contrasting two things:

1. **The "today" baseline** — Valtech's existing
   [Product Competency Framework](#appendix-the-today-baseline-valtech-product-competency-framework):
   4 themes and 16 competencies that describe where PMs are expected to be *now*.
2. **The future vector** — the role-shift dimensions below, which describe how those
   competencies evolve toward the **Product Builder / AI-PM**.

The product reads live client context, locates where on the baseline the client is
operating, and uses the shift dimensions to recommend concrete **moves** — both a
**mindset shift** (how to see the work) and a **concrete skill** (a move to practise) —
that pull the PM toward Product Builder *for this specific client, engagement, and phase*.

## How to read each shift dimension

Each dimension describes a shift from the **old PM** posture to the **Product Builder**
posture. For each, we list:

- **Anchors to** — which competency theme(s) from the today-baseline this shift evolves.
- **Maturity tells** — concrete signals in real client text (job postings, ways-of-working
  notes, intel) that suggest the client genuinely operates this way.
- **Hype tells** — language that adopts the vocabulary of change without the substance.
  These are the "breeze merchant" markers.

## 1. Discovery → Sensing

**Old PM:** Runs interviews and synthesizes insight on a quarterly cadence.
**Product Builder:** Uses AI to sense signal continuously — pulls from support logs,
usage data, and models, synthesizing in hours, not sprints.

- **Anchors to:** Discovery (Using quantitative data, Using qualitative data, Hypothesis
  and assumption identification).
- **Maturity tells:** continuous discovery; data/instrumentation referenced concretely;
  "we move fast on signal"; named telemetry or research ops.
- **Hype tells:** "data-driven" as a buzzword with no instrumentation behind it;
  "AI-powered insights" with no method described.

## 2. Spec → Build

**Old PM:** Writes the requirements, hands off, waits.
**Product Builder:** Prototypes the thing — builds clickable or working artifacts with
AI tooling to think *through* making, not just describing.

- **Anchors to:** Execution (Articulating requirements); Discovery (Experimentation, Ideation).
- **Maturity tells:** "PMs prototype"; design–eng–PM blur; references to vibe-coding or
  internal tool-building; PMs shipping their own experiments.
- **Hype tells:** "PM should be technical" with no evidence of a building culture;
  rigid spec-handoff process dressed up in modern language.

## 3. Roadmaps → Bets

**Old PM:** Defends a 12-month roadmap.
**Product Builder:** Frames work as falsifiable bets, kills fast, and lets the roadmap
stay emergent because AI collapses the cost of trying.

- **Anchors to:** Execution (Roadmapping, Prioritisation and managing trade-offs);
  Vision & strategy (Product strategy, Defining success metrics).
- **Maturity tells:** outcome language; "experiments"; demonstrated tolerance for killing
  work; bets framed with explicit hypotheses.
- **Hype tells:** "agile" theatre; a feature-factory roadmap dressed in OKR language.

## 4. Managing teams → Orchestrating systems

**Old PM:** Coordinates humans.
**Product Builder:** Designs workflows where agents do real work — and knows where human
judgment must stay.

- **Anchors to:** Execution (Running effective teams); Consulting (Modelling & coaching
  best practice).
- **Maturity tells:** "agentic workflows"; AI embedded in the actual PDLC; talk of evals,
  guardrails, and human-in-the-loop boundaries.
- **Hype tells:** "we use AI internally" meaning someone has ChatGPT open; agent-washing.

## 5. Hype-resistance (the meta-dimension)

**Old PM / breeze merchant:** Adopts the vocabulary of change without the substance.
**Product Builder:** Can tell the difference and spends effort only where it compounds.

This dimension is **not scored from the client text directly**. It is scored from how
cleanly the other four dimensions separated into signal vs. hype. It is the breezemerchant
lens itself — the discipline of distinguishing what's real from what's noise.

## Editing notes

- Keep dimensions to a small, opinionated set. Resist sprawl — more dimensions dilute the POV.
- Tells should be **concrete and quotable**, not abstract. "We run weekly agent-evals" beats
  "uses AI maturely."
- This file is versioned. Treat changes to it as changes to the product's brain.
- Each recommended **move** has two parts: a **mindset shift** (how to see the work) and
  a **concrete skill** (a specific thing to practise). Keep both — mindset without a move
  is a platitude; a skill without mindset is box-ticking.

## Appendix: the "today" baseline (Valtech Product Competency Framework)

This is where Valtech expects PMs to be **today**. The shift dimensions above describe how
each of these evolves toward Product Builder. Source: Product Competency Framework 2024.

| Theme | Competencies |
|---|---|
| **Vision & strategy** | Vision and proposition design · Product strategy · Defining success metrics |
| **Discovery** | Hypothesis and assumption identification · Using quantitative data · Using qualitative data · Experimentation · Ideation |
| **Execution** | Running effective teams · Roadmapping · Articulating requirements · Prioritisation and managing trade-offs |
| **Consulting** | Engagement delivery · Managing stakeholders · Modelling & coaching best practice · Driving account growth |
